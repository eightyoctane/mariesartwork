Making Proposals at BMA

Initiatives are started by BMA owners/residents as ideas and recommendations to solve problems, meet needs, and improve overall lifestyle at Blue Mountain Airpark. Initiatives don't get anything done however. Initiatives are just a mechanism to garner support for a [good] idea. Anyone can create an initiative, but remember, if you create one, your name is attached to it.

NOTE:   Anything associated with initiatives and proposals on the website is informal, final voting must be submitted in writing to the Board of Directors.

Initiative Stage

  1. Open an initiative (this screen)
  2. Submit at least one proposal (multiples preferred)
  3. Get the votes you need to get the initiative approved before the voting deadline arrives

Each initiative is associated with one or more proposals. Later, when votes are cast for an initiative, they are cast with a comment. This gives the voter the opportunity to state which proposal is favored. Votes and comments are public and can be viewed by everyone in the community while the voting phase is in progress. Votes and comments can be changed by each casting voter, at any time during the voting period.

Be sure to leave enough time for voting. Once the deadline arrives, the initiative will be set to "approved", "rejected", or "uncertainq" . The term "uncertainq" just means that we never established a voting quorum (>30% of the possible voters voted), and the initiative closed due to lack of interest.

If the initiative results in "rejected" or "uncertainq", it is essentially closed. Once the initiative closes, it must be re-approved by the Board, and reopened by a Board Member. If the initiative is "accepted", the next phase is the "Committee Phase".

Committee Phase

The board president or vice president has the authority to create a new committee and assign a chairman. Once the chairman is assigned, the new chairman manage the committee, and will add committee members with defined responsibilities.

  1. Present ideas/proposals (face-to-face) to the board and residents formally and informally
  2. Help build the committee (and lead the committee) and participate on the committee
  3. Identify the most accepted proposal and start work
  4. Take responsibility for getting the project done

Initiatives Page Information (help with this screen)

Initiative Name > just an identifier, pick something that is indicative. (Ex: BMA Equipment and Tool Storage Building)

Short Description > (Ex: We need a storage building to house the BMA lawn mowers and miscellaneous tools)

Abstract: Your sales pitch

"I have a proposal ready" > If you check this box you will get another screen after this one that enables you to upload a detailed proposal (this should be a pdf or doc file). It's a great idea to submit a detailed proposal right now, even before the initiative is approved. This will eliminate all questions regarding whether or not you are serious about the initiative. Anybody can submit proposals before the voting deadline.

Suggested Chairman: If you select this, the person you are volunteering will get an email indicating that you volunteered them.

Voting Deadline > This is the number of days that the selected group has available to cast a vote and comment on the news page to indicate their support. This doesn't mean it's their responsibility to vote by that time. It's your responsibility to remind (hound) them to make a decision. After the deadline, the Initiative will be set to it's new state.
