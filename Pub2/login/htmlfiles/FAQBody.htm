The ALCC Response Letter

What constitutes a "proposal" that goes to the ALCC?

For best and quickest results, the Construction Proposal "Packet" should include the information outlined in the Project Suggestions - Construction and Landscaping at Blue Mountain Airpark document. The property owner/resident should send a letter or email to the ALCC chairman which references the packet as the formal proposal, and should include a proposed starting construction date. Upon receipt of the packet, the ALCC has 30 days to respond with approval, or requested changes required to get approval. The email or letter is a critical part of the process and "pulls the trigger" on the 30 day response.

Who officially represents Blue Mountain Airpark?

Blue Mountain Airpark is represented by the incorporated and elected Board of Directors who make all final decisions regarding the Covenants. Typically the Board delegates the implementation plans to a Blue Mountain Committee. The Covenants address the Architectural and Landscaping Control Committee (ALCC) specifically. The ALCC collects and reviews Property Construction and Landscaping plans using the Covenants, then reports to the Board and requests that they either send the Property Owner a letter of approval, or itemize the changes which need to be made to bring the Construction plan into compliance. Other committees are organized to address and bring initiatives to fruition.

How do I contact the ALCC?

Marie House is currently the chairman of the ALCC. Please email her with any questions at mariesartwork@yahoo.com

Are there "rules" at Blue Mountain, and if so, how do they affect me?

From a Property Owner standpoint, the "rules" at Blue Mountain benefit us all by defining a "minimum" living standard within the Airpark (for example, someone cannot build a shack in the airpark, store a tractor ourdoors or build a chicken coop next to your house). These "rules" are defined in the DECLARATION OF PROTECTIVE AND RESTRICTIVE COVENANTS FOR "BLUE MOUNTAIN AIR PARK" and were pointed out at the time you purchased your property. Every Blue Mountain lot is subject to the Covenants.

Enforcement is addressed specifically in Part I item 35 of the Covenants, and involves legal action as well as a lien on the subject property.

Can I rent out my house?

Rental Guidelines for Blue Mountain Airpark

 

  • Owners are responsible for a qualified tenant.
  • Owners are responsible for the tenant’s actions.
  • Federal and State guidelines must be followed.
  • Covenants must be read, signed, and abided by the prospective tenant, where applicable.
  • Blue Mountain Association Board must be made aware of the prospect of renting in writing or by e-mail and meet with the prospective tenant to orient them to the covenants and common property rules.
  • Prefer that the prospective tenant have a pilot’s license, or an aeronautical background, or a significant interest in aviation.
  • Lease to purchase agreement most favorable.
  • Leases are to be a minimum of one year.
  • No house is to be built with the sole purpose of renting.

 Blue Mountain Airpark Common Property Rules

  • Owners are responsible for their guests activities while in the airpark.
  • No play activity on runway or overrun with out adult supervision.
  • Inform other owners when you want to leave the gate open for guests.
  • Inform other owners when you are expecting a large group of guests due to increase in traffic in the area.
  • Care must be taken when giving out the gate code for security reasons.
  • Airstrip operations are subject to normal FAA rules and regulations.
  • All common areas are to remain free from any toys or debris.
  • All trash must be picked up and disposed of in the owner’s receptacle. (It is not to be left in the cans at the picnic area unless function is association sponsored.)
