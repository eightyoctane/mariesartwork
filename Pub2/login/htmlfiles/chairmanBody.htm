Committees (12/2011)

1. Architectural and Landscaping Control Committee (ALCC) (Marie House)

Description:

The Blue Mountain Airpark Vice President role is a unique position. According to the BMA Covenants, the VP is responsible for representing the Board, and also plays the role of Architectural Committee Chairman. What does this mean? The role is significant because the Chairman is the "last word", and responsible for maintaining a minimum quality building/living standard at BMA according to the Restrictive Covenants. The BMA Restrictive Covenants protect substantial homeowner investments in property and housing within the airpark. Owners involved in new construction are required to submit final building plans and obtain approval from the Architectural Committee before proceeding. Any changes during construction must also be submitted and receive approval. Everything goes smoothly unless there is an undocumented change. It's not easy or desirable to remind owners that these rules are in place. Communication to all, timeliness and fairness to all owners is a critical part of this job.

Architectural Committee
Architectural Approval Process

 
Upon receipt of formal plans from BMA Owner/Resident
 
Within 30 days
  1. Receive plans from the resident who is proposing the new structure. Insure that the date and time of receipt is recorded
  2. Send an email to the person in 1 above indicating the date and time received.
  3. Insure that the other architectural committee members have a copy of the plans (via snail mail, email, or the website)
  4. Confer with at least one other knowledgeable committee member (preferably two), regarding the plan details
  5. Get approval from those consulted in 3 above. Approval must be in writing or in email
  6. Fill out the architectural approval form and have it signed by either the Vice President or the President
  7. Personally deliver the letter to the person described in 1 above (if possible).
