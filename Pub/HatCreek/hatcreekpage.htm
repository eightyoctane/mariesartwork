Hat Creek, California

 

My Dear friend, Gary, whom I have known since I was 13, suggested that he could fly me to Hat Creek, California and I could paint while he fly fished. 

My first journey to Hat Creek was an adventure. After flying from Chattanooga to San Francisco, we got into a small plane and flew 2 hours to Redding, CA. From there we drove to Hat Creek and then hiked into a remote location carrying my 2 x 4 foot easel, paints and canvas. The cat tails were as tall as I am and you could hear the water moving slowly by.

WOW - I flew 3,000 miles to be able to stand on the river's edge in Hat Creek. What a chance of a lifetime!

At first, I just sat in awe of the peaceful, serene landscape that unfolded before my eyes - so different from the Tennessee.

 

The flight and drive to Hat Creek had given me the opportunity to see just how rapidly the terrain changes in Northern California. Mount Shasta with her snow capped peaks and the vast landscape just won my heart. I started getting that old feeling I get when I need to spill what I have in my head and heart on the canvas.

 

It was early afternoon when I arrived on the river's edge and got busy setting up. I thought for a while and then decided I would paint my interpretation of the Hat Creek fishing hole. From time to time a fish would slap the water and I could look off in the distance and see the beautiful mountains. The light was changing as the afternoon went by.

 

I have made several trips back to this area and have photographed many beautiful areas to paint in my studio. When I remove myself from the location, I am able to put more emotions into my paintings.
