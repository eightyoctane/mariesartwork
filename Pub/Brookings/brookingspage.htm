Brookings, Oregon

 

I flew into San Jose, got off the plane and headed to Gary's 1948 Bonanza to load it for a trip to Brookings, Oregon. What an adventure trying to get all of my art supplies in his small plane.

Gary had called ahead and was told the airport in Brookings was "socked in", meaning fogged in. SO....... we had to fly as close as we could get then rent a car and drive to Brookings, Oregon. This change of plans allowed me to see the Redwood Forest, and as we drove up the coastline I saw just how powerful the Pacific Ocean is with the waves crashing against the rocks. After just experiencing the Gulf in Destin, Florida, the Pacific Ocean was absolutely the most powerful ocean I have experienced. After hiking up to perch on a cliff, I set up my canvas on my easel which took some doing in the wind and mist of the waves breaking. The Pacific was literally surrounding me.

I chose a 5 x 6 foot canvas to get the experience of the vast surroundings, standing on a bluff overlooking the ocean all around me.

To feel the wind and see the wind moving in the seagrass - it was pure SERENITY that I felt painting up that high over the ocean with a bird's eye view of the beach.

I thank my friend, Gary, for giving me the opportunity to fly into these remote areas and experience what beauty nature truly has!
